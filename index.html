Project Scope

Literature Survey

Sri Lanka is recognized as an important nesting ground for five of the world’s seven sea turtle species, including Green, Olive Ridley, Hawksbill, Loggerhead, and Leatherback turtles. Coastal areas such as Kosgoda, Rekawa, and Bundala play a significant role in supporting these endangered species.

However, sea turtles face numerous threats. Natural predators such as dogs, birds, crabs, and monitor lizards destroy nests and prey on hatchlings. Additionally, environmental challenges like dehydration and disorientation reduce hatchling survival rates. Human activities further worsen the situation through illegal egg collection, poaching of adult turtles, and coastal development.

Habitat degradation due to climate change, rising sea levels, and artificial lighting also disrupt nesting behavior. Furthermore, fisheries bycatch causes significant mortality among turtles, as they get trapped in fishing nets.

Recent studies highlight the importance of integrating Artificial Intelligence (AI) and computer vision for wildlife monitoring. AI-based systems can detect patterns, track animal behavior, and identify threats in real time. These technologies provide a scalable and efficient solution for conservation challenges.

Research Gap

Following gaps are identified in existing solutions:

Lack of Automated Monitoring Systems

Most current monitoring activities are conducted manually by conservation staff. This approach is labor intensive, time consuming, and not scalable for large coastal areas.

Absence of Real-Time Threat Detection

Existing systems do not provide real-time detection of predators or illegal human activities. As a result, responses to threats are often delayed.

Limited Use of AI in Disease Detection

There is very limited implementation of AI-based solutions for detecting diseases such as fibropapillomatosis in sea turtles, especially using small datasets.

Inefficient Hatchery Management

Hatchery monitoring is mostly manual and lacks intelligent systems to detect abnormal hatchling behavior or improper tank conditions.

Lack of Integrated Environmental Analysis

Current solutions do not effectively combine environmental data such as weather conditions and shoreline changes to predict risks to nests.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How can an intelligent system be developed to monitor sea turtle nesting activities, detect threats, identify diseases, and support conservation efforts in real time?

Sea turtle conservation in Sri Lanka is challenged by increasing environmental threats, human interference, and inefficiencies in traditional monitoring methods. The absence of automated and integrated systems limits the ability to respond quickly and effectively to these challenges.


Product Demonstration - Solution

Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is an AI-driven monitoring and protection system designed to enhance sea turtle conservation efforts through automation and real-time intelligence.

The system utilizes camera-based surveillance installed along coastal areas to continuously monitor turtle activities. Advanced computer vision algorithms are used to detect sea turtles, identify nesting behaviors, and accurately determine nest locations. Once a nest is identified, its geographic coordinates are recorded, and alerts are sent to conservation authorities.

In addition to monitoring turtles, the system detects potential threats such as predators and unauthorized human presence. Real-time alerts enable rapid response to prevent nest destruction. The system also incorporates a disease detection module that analyzes images of turtles to identify conditions such as fibropapillomatosis using Few-Shot Learning techniques.

Furthermore, the system includes a hatchery monitoring component that tracks hatchlings, identifies species, and detects abnormal behavior patterns. Environmental factors such as weather conditions and shoreline changes are also analyzed to predict risks and provide early warnings.

Overall, the solution aims to provide a comprehensive, automated, and intelligent platform for improving the effectiveness of sea turtle conservation in Sri Lanka.

Research Objectives

Detection and Monitoring of Sea Turtle Nesting Behavior

The first objective is to develop an AI-powered solution to detect and monitor sea turtle nesting activities along coastal areas in Sri Lanka. The system will identify turtle movements and nesting behaviors such as excavation, egg laying, and nest covering using computer vision techniques. In addition, the system will accurately determine nest locations using geo-tagging and provide real-time alerts to conservation authorities.

Identification of Threats to Sea Turtle Nests

The second objective is to identify potential threats such as predators and unauthorized human activities using object detection algorithms. The system will provide real-time alerts to enable rapid intervention and improve nest survival rates.

Detection and Classification of Sea Turtle Diseases

The third objective is to develop a disease detection module to identify conditions such as fibropapillomatosis using Few-Shot Learning. The system will differentiate diseases from similar conditions and provide care recommendations.

Monitoring and Management of Hatchery Environments

The fourth objective is to monitor hatchlings using object detection and tracking. The system will detect species, monitor behavior, and identify abnormalities such as low activity or mixed species in tanks.

Environmental Risk Analysis

The fifth objective is to integrate environmental data such as weather, tides, and shoreline changes to predict risks like flooding and erosion. The system will generate alerts to support proactive conservation actions.

Methodology

The proposed Sea Turtle Conservation Monitoring System consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. Sea Turtle Nesting Detection and Monitoring
  2. Threat Detection and Identification (Predators and Human Activities)
  3. Disease Detection and Identification (Fibropapillomatosis)
  4. Hatchery Managment and Behavior Monitoring
  5. Environmental Risk Analysis

The proposed system is implemented as an AI-driven multi-module surveillance platform that integrates computer vision, deep learning, and geospatial analysis to monitor and protect sea turtles in coastal areas. Initially, camera devices are deployed to continuously capture images and video streams of nesting sites. These inputs are processed using deep learning models such as YOLO for detecting sea turtles, nesting behavior, and potential threats including predators and unauthorized human activity. Once a nesting event is detected, the system records the geolocation using GPS/geo-tagging and sends real-time alerts to conservation authorities.

In parallel, a disease detection module analyzes turtle images using Few-Shot Learning to identify conditions such as fibropapillomatosis and distinguish them from similar appearances. A hatchery monitoring module tracks hatchling movement and behavior using object detection and tracking techniques to identify abnormalities and species mixing. Additionally, environmental data such as weather conditions, tides, and shoreline changes are integrated to assess risks to nesting sites and generate predictive alerts. All processed data is centralized in a cloud-based system, enabling real-time visualization, monitoring, and decision support for conservation stakeholders.
